70 Years With SP

SP at 70: From Humble Beginnings to a Future of Excellence

What began in a humble five-storey building on Prince Edward Road grew into Singapore's largest polytechnic—a sprawling campus spanning 38 hectares of lush greenery and over 230,000 esteemed alumni. Our 70-year milestone in 2024 is a moment of reflection and celebration, but also the beginning of our next era, with more to discover and more to accomplish. This is SP at 70.

SP70 MOMENTS

POLITE Staff Appreciation Day
On October 31, 2023, Singapore Polytechnic launched its 70th anniversary book, For All Ages, Singapore Polytechnic At Seventy, during POLITE Staff Appreciation Day. The book details SP's history and achievements and kicks off its 70th anniversary celebrations. Minister for Education Mr. Chan Chun Sing was the Guest-of-Honour.
SP Open House 2024
The SP campus was lively during our 70th Anniversary Open House this year. Visitors enjoyed school tours of our state-of-the-art facilities, lively CCA performances at the Plaza, and a special drone display as part of the SP Industry & Innovation Showcase.
SP70th Anniversary Music Festival 2024
For SP’s 70th Anniversary, we hosted a music festival at SP. Despite the rain, thousands enjoyed performances by local artists Evan Low, Rangga Jones, and Marian Carmel. The festival highlighted the importance of youth mental wellness and showed how music can support mental well-being.
SP70 Charity Golf and Gala Dinner
At the SP 70th Anniversary Charity Golf and Gala Dinner, Principal and CEO Mr. Soh Wai Wah welcomed guests. Over $2 million was raised for the Singapore Polytechnic Graduates' Guild (SPGG) Education Fund, providing more than 100 annual bursaries of $2,500 for students in need. A symbolic $100,000 cheque was also presented to President Tharman Shanmugaratnam for the President’s Challenge to support social causes.

SP's MILESTONES

On 27th October 1954, the Legislative Council passed the Singapore Ordinance, leading to the creation of Singapore’s first polytechnic.

Dr. C. A. Hart led the Colombo Plan team to study the development of advanced courses. The University of Singapore explored the feasibility of establishing a faculty of technology and granting university degrees to Polytechnic graduates.

Building of Dover Road campus began.

All full-time Technician Diploma courses have been restructured and updated to meet high technology requirements.

SP became the first poly to introduce the Diploma in Optometry.

Singapore Polytechnic turned 50 and became the first poly to produce a perfume which sold out within 9 days.

SP celebrates its 60th anniversary, launches the Perfumery and Cosmetic Science Centre, and a new five-storey design school was established.

SP celebrates its 70th birthday!
